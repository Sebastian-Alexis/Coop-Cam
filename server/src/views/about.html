Meet the Chickens

Our family has 6 chickens in total, all of which are about 4 months old. Each one has their own unique personality and brings joy to our backyard coop.

Marshmallow

Rooster

Marshmallow is a rooster, and was originally named when he was a much purer shade of white. Since then, he has grown darker features, giving him a nice look. He is generally pretty comfortable with people, but if you try to pick up any of his hens, he can get pretty protective, and even try chasing or pecking at you.

Charcoal

Hen

Charcoal is a hen named after her distinctive black/grey body which transitions into a vibrant orange on her next, like glowing coals. She loves following her sisters, and enjoys pecking at anything green.

UV

Hen

UV is a hen named after ultraviolet light, as her feathers have a strangle shimmery black quality. She is pretty unique in that she has feathers on her feet, which is a trait that I've heared is from the Brahma species, but I'm not entirely sure what exact species she is. If you can tell, let me know!

Toasty

Hen

Toasty is one of two brown chickens, but is the only one that does not have a wattle (the fleshly red thing that hangs from a chickens beak). This is really the only difference between her and her sister, who is also a hen, and for a while I couldn't actually tell them apart.

Rusty

Hen

Rusty is the only brown hen that has a wattle, and is named partially after her color, but also after her personality, which I can only really describe as rusty. She is very curious, but also very quick to run away from you. If standing still, she will look up at you with a tilted head, which is pretty funny to see.

Cheeto Puff

Hen

Cheeto Puff is a hen named after her orange color, which is a bit more vibrant than the other brown hens. She is also named after the way she relaxes, which is curling up into a puff of featers, with only the very top of her head visible.

Setup

Our coop is built in our backyard, featuring an outer enclosed area where the chickens can roam freely during the day, and an inner henhouse where they sleep safely at night. We usually open up the enclosure during the day, so they can roam around the yard, and at night, they naturally walk back into the coop, which we lock to keep out predators.

Food

Our feeder is a bucket that has a neat little attachment that keeps the food dry, and helps keep the chickens from making a mess. We fill it with high quality chicken feed, and it lasts for about 2 weeks per fill. One reason you may see the chickens eating leaves or searching for bugs is because while the feed is great for keeping them fit and healthy, I personally don't think they like it too much, just like how we humans need all our nutrients, but don't want to eat only health food. We also will sometimes give the hens a bit of out leftovers, things like rice or bread, which they love. You just have to be careful giving chickens scraps, because too much can make them sick. There has to be the health food of the chicken feed alongside the junk food of our scraps.

Water

Our water is also a bucket with these really clever attachments drilled in. What these do is add a couple tiny beak-sized areas that fill with just enough water, which keeps the chickens from wasting water, and still let them drink as much as they want. A really clever design, in my opinion.

Henhouse

The henhouse is a wooden box that we painted red and white (classic henhouse colors). It has a little door that we can open and close, although we always leave it open because the main door of the coop is the only one that really needs to be locked to keep out predators. We have a roosting bar where all the chickens can sit at night, and in the future, we are going to add specific areas where chickens can securely lay eggs.
